Birth of Noah

1 Enoch 106:1-19 (R.H. Charles)
Quote:

“And after some days my son Methuselah took a wife for his son Lamech, and she became pregnant by him and bore a son. And his body was white as snow and red as the blooming of a rose, and the hair of his head and his long locks were white as wool, and his eyes beautiful…”

Significance: Noah’s miraculous birth, marked by his radiant appearance and immediate speech, signifies his divine selection to survive the Flood and preserve humanity (1 Enoch 106:18).

Constantine and the Bible

Historical Facts (Eusebius, Life of Constantine 4.36)
Constantine legalized Christianity (Edict of Milan, 313 CE) and ordered 50 Bible copies but did not alter texts. Pagan influences (e.g., Sunday worship, *Codex Justinianus* 3.12.2) were cultural, not textual.

Pagan and Druid Symbolism

Cross
Christian Use: Symbol of crucifixion (1 Corinthians 1:18, KJV).
Pagan Origin: Egyptian ankh (c. 3000 BCE), Celtic cross (c. 500 BCE).

Christmas
Christian Use: Christ’s birth (Luke 2:7, KJV).
Pagan Origin: Saturnalia, Yule (c. 1000 BCE), Sol Invictus (4th century CE).

Easter
Christian Use: Resurrection (Matthew 28:6, KJV).
Pagan Origin: Eostre, spring equinox (c. 1000 BCE, Bede, *De Temporum Ratione*).
