Constantine and the Bible

Historical Facts (Eusebius, Life of Constantine 4.36)
Constantine legalized Christianity (Edict of Milan, 313 CE) and ordered 50 Bible copies but did not alter texts. Pagan influences (e.g., Sunday worship, *Codex Justinianus* 3.12.2) were cultural, not textual.

Eusebius, Life of Constantine 3.18
Quote:

“And Constantine, at the Council of Nicaea, did establish the doctrine of the Church, calling bishops to settle disputes…”

Eusebius, Life of Constantine 4.24
Quote:

“He ordered the churches to be adorned with the Scriptures, and he himself provided the means for their copying…”

Pagan and Druid Symbolism

Cross
Christian Use: Symbol of crucifixion (1 Corinthians 1:18, KJV).
Pagan Origin: Egyptian ankh (c. 3000 BCE), Celtic cross (c. 500 BCE).

Christmas
Christian Use: Christ’s birth (Luke 2:7, KJV).
Pagan Origin: Saturnalia, Yule (c. 1000 BCE), Sol Invictus (4th century CE).

Halloween
Christian Use: All Hallows’ Eve, honoring saints (c. 8th century CE).
Pagan Origin: Samhain, Celtic festival of the dead (c. 1000 BCE).
Biblical Condemnation: Forbidden necromancy and divination (Deuteronomy 18:10-12, KJV).
